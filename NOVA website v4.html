About NOVA

How We Vet Talent

We don't operate a directory. Every professional in the NOVA network has passed a rigorous, multi-stage evaluation – fewer than 15% of applicants are admitted.

Our 5-Stage Vetting Process

1

Application Screening

Every applicant submits a detailed profile including education, work history, portfolio samples, and structured written responses to scenario-based questions. Reviewed manually – no auto-approvals.

2

Technical Assessment

Role-specific tests designed by senior practitioners. Developers face real coding challenges. Marketers build strategy briefs. Designers submit case studies. Analysts work through real datasets. Timed and proctored.

3

Live Interview Panel

Structured video interview with NOVA staff and a subject-matter expert. We assess communication clarity, problem-solving under pressure, English proficiency, and professional conduct.

4

Trial Project

Final candidates complete a paid micro-project – a real, abbreviated version of client work. Tests delivery under deadline, quality, responsiveness to feedback, and independent operation.

5

Background & Reference Check

Identity verified, credentials confirmed, professional references checked directly. Contractors sign a code of conduct, NDA framework, and compliance agreement before admission.

What We Look For

Technical Depth

Real working knowledge – not surface familiarity. We care about what candidates can actually build, analyze, or execute.

Communication Standard

Remote work depends on excellent communication. We assess written and spoken English, responsiveness, and ability to ask the right clarifying questions.

Reliability Track Record

We probe specifically for past missed deadlines or incomplete projects – and weight reliability heavily in our scoring.

Professionalism & Integrity

References are checked. Conduct standards are set in writing. We remove professionals from the network immediately for any violation of client trust.

"Fewer than 15% of applicants are admitted. We built this standard because our clients' businesses depend on it."

Ongoing Quality Monitoring

Vetting doesn't end at admission. Every completed project is rated by both client and contractor. We monitor NPS scores, on-time delivery rates, and communication quality continuously. Professionals who fall below standards are placed on review or removed entirely.

Our Story

Our Mission

NOVA exists to close the gap between Africa's rising talent generation and the world's unmet demand for skilled, affordable, and reliable remote professionals.

The Problem We're Solving

Small and medium-sized businesses in the US, UK, and EU need high-skill talent to grow – but local hiring is prohibitive. Meanwhile, thousands of highly educated professionals in Nigeria and across Africa are underemployed and overlooked by global markets.

Freelance platforms place the entire burden on the client: vetting, communication, contracts, payments, quality control. For a busy founder, that's a full-time job on top of everything else.

Why Africa, Why Now

Nigeria alone produces over 60,000 STEM graduates annually. The continent's tech talent base is growing faster than anywhere else in the world – fueled by a young population, expanding universities, and a proliferating ecosystem of bootcamps and tech hubs. English proficiency is high. Motivation is exceptional.

Favorable currency exchange rates mean a highly qualified Nigerian professional can deliver equivalent value at 40–70% below the cost of a Western hire – not because their work is worth less, but because purchasing power parity creates a structural advantage that benefits both sides.

"We're not arbitraging cheap labor. We're unlocking overlooked excellence – and making it accessible to businesses that need it most."

NOVA's Role

We operate as a managed service firm. We take accountability – for quality, timing, communication, contracts, and payments. Clients work with NOVA, and NOVA delivers. Our professionals don't navigate confusing international payment systems alone; NOVA handles the infrastructure so they can focus entirely on their craft.
